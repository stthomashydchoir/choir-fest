Psalm 149
P:

Praise the Lord. Sing to the Lord a new song, His praise in the assembly of His faithful people.

C:

Let Israel rejoice in their Maker; let the people of Zion be glad in their King.

P:

Let them praise His name with dancing and make music to Him with timbrel and harp.

C:

For the Lord takes delight in His people; He crowns the humble with victory.

P:

Let His faithful people rejoice in this honor and sing for joy on their beds.

C:

May the praise of God be in their mouths and a double-edged sword in their hands,

P:

To inflict vengeance on the nations and punishment on the peoples,

C:

To bind their kings with fetters, their nobles with shackles of iron,

P:

To carry out the sentence written against them—

All:

This is the glory of all His faithful people. Praise the Lord!

Prayer for The Choir Fest
P:

Almighty God, our Rock and Selah, we gather today to make a joyful noise that shakes the foundations of injustice. As we raise our voices, we ask that our worship be more than melody—may it be a mighty river of justice and an ever-flowing stream of righteousness.

We pray for every participating choir, their leaders, and the musicians, that they would be vessels of peace, carrying the message of love and truth beyond these walls. Bless our guests, friends, and the congregation gathered here, that the harmony we create would bring healing to broken situations.

Lord, make this fest turning point, where our songs inspire action, our voices unite for the vulnerable, and this event brings peace to our community and blessing to many.

C:

Amen

English
Closing congregational hymn · Henry Francis Lyte

Abide with me; fast falls the eventide; The darkness deepens; Lord with me abide! When other helpers fail and comforts flee, Help of the helpless, O abide with me.

Stanza 2

Swift to its close ebbs out life's little day; Earth's joys grow dim; its glories pass away; Change and decay in all around I see; O Thou who changest not, abide with me.
